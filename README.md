# VBFDD-Agent: Vehicle Battery Fault Detection and Diagnosis Agent

This repository provides the open-source results associated with our study on **VBFDD-Agent**, an LLM-empowered framework for electric vehicle battery fault detection, diagnosis, and maintenance recommendation generation.

![image](https://github.com/sjtu-chan-joey/Battery-Timer/blob/main/Figures/lora.png)

## Overview

With the rapid development of electric vehicles, battery fault detection and maintenance decision support have become increasingly important for safe and reliable operation. Traditional battery fault detection methods mainly rely on numerical monitoring signals and usually output fault labels, anomaly scores, or alarm results. However, such outputs are often difficult to directly use in real-world maintenance scenarios.

To address this issue, our study explores a new way of connecting battery monitoring signals with language-based reasoning. We transform vehicle battery operating data into **mechanism-informed descriptive texts**, so that battery states, abnormal patterns, and potential risks can be expressed in a readable and interpretable form. Based on these descriptive texts, VBFDD-Agent further generates maintenance-oriented diagnostic outputs and disposal recommendations.

## What We Release

This repository includes:

- Mechanism-informed descriptive text modeling results generated from vehicle battery monitoring signals.
- Generated maintenance recommendation results produced by VBFDD-Agent.
- Public source information used to compile the local maintenance knowledge base.
- Example outputs for battery fault diagnosis and operation-maintenance decision support.

## Purpose

The released materials are intended to support future research on:

- Electric vehicle battery fault detection and diagnosis.
- Battery operation and maintenance corpus construction.
- LLM-empowered battery health management.
- Text-signal collaborative modeling for battery systems.
- Human-AI collaborative maintenance decision support.

## Notes

The released data and results are provided for academic research purposes. The maintenance recommendations generated by VBFDD-Agent are intended for decision support and should not replace expert inspection or official maintenance procedures in safety-critical applications.
